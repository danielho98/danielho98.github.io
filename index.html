RESEARCH

I'm currently a researcher at the UC Berkeley HART (Hand-Assistive Robotics Technologies) Lab. We are a computer science and electronics-based research lab under the EECS department at Cal. Our focus this semester has involved tackling the processing of medical images with an automated approach. We are working with OpenCV in Python and using computer vision techniques to process images in a way that will save a significant number of man-hours, as the proecss is currently manual and a tedious process. We are also exploring the use of neural nets to solve other problems we are currently looking at.

In high school during the summer of my senior year, I was a researcher in the Mullins Lab at University of Pennsylvania. Our primary field of study was developmental biology, where we used zebrafish as a model organism. My PI, Benjamin Tajer, focused on exploring the viability of chimeric receptors in the zebrafish. We tracked early stages of development in the gestation period of zebrafish, hoping to gain further insight in the development stages of humans. My work and wet lab skills involved HRM (high resolution melt) analysis, western blot, Qiagen purification on large samples, DNA amplification, DNA genome analysis (post-sequencing) and more. The Mullins lab operates in one of the largest zebrafish facilities in the country.

Research has always been a point of interest for me outside of the classroom. There's usually a learning curve involved with research -- becoming an expert in your topic of research and knowing how to execute research efficiently. Progress may not always be shown linearly, and this can get frustrating sometimes, but it's something I see as worthwhile and rewarding.

PROJECTS

BearMaps is a project similar to Google maps. It can take in latitude and longitude coordinates as an input and it will output an appropriate map of Berkeley scaled to the proper resolution and size. For it's direction functionality, the program creates a graph out of all the streets and locations in Berkeley, and uses A* algorithm (a directed version of Dijkstra's algorithm) to find the shortest path to the destination. It also has a search function that utilizes the Trie data structure to output all possible results given a search query.

Gl0w is an arduino-based hackathon project that I created last year. It features a temperature sensor and an RGB LED that displays different colors depending on the temperature read on the sensor. The picture seen here on this website is a picture of this project.

Earlier this year, I started working with Jacob Alexander on his project KLL, the Keyboard Layout Language. It's a custom firmware project for keyboards that aims to create a universal firmware platform for keyboards. You can find the code for this project here: https://github.com/kiibohd/kll

KEYBOARDS

There's an almost cult-like following of people interested in computer keyboards as a hobby, and I am one of them!

I've lost count of the number of keyboards I own, but I know that I own at least 30 keyboards currently, and I estimate the current value of my collection to be worth over $5000! (that's more than what my car is worth)

The amount of keyboard enthusiasts has grown to be very large in recent years. It consists of a tight-knit community of people who are interested in a niche product, but the appeal has expanded widely. In high school, I wrote full-length product reviews of new keyboards and posted these reviews online, gathering thousands of views for each review. I also worked with employees at Massdrop, a once-startup company in San Francisco that sold enthusiast-grade items including keyboards.

Earlier this year, I worked with Jacob Alexander to organize a meetup for keyboard enthusiasts. We hosted the event at Facebook HQ and there were over 100 attendees at the meetup, and featured food, cool prizes and free t-shirts for those who came.

I was also recently interviewed by a man working on writing a book detailing the entire history of keyboards, and he asked me about the keyboards in the modern era and about keyboards as a hobby for many people today.
